Frenzy

Zach Reed

Zach is noise in the best way. Laughter spilling out of him like it can’t be contained. He jokes mid-round, trash-talks with a wink, loses his skateboard under the couch and never looks for it again.

He’s talented in that unfair way—hands that win championships, fingers that sketch cartoons between matches, a brain that just gets rhythm whether it’s CS timing or piano chords. But the real reason people orbit him? He’s kind. Loud kindness. Loyal-to-the-bone kindness. The kind that defends his friends like it’s instinct, not choice.

Being raised by a single dad gave him this quiet core of responsibility he pretends not to have. And he had an older sister, but he don't talk about that.
